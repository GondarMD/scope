AI systems increasingly expand from assistive tools into authority-bearing organisational roles. This expansion rarely occurs through deliberate design. Instead, authority accumulates incrementally through additional integrations, broader data access, and expanding operational responsibilities. While each individual change may appear justified, the sum of these changes often results in increasing organisational exposure that remains largely unobserved until failure occurs.

Existing governance frameworks provide valuable guidance on risk management, accountability, compliance, and operational oversight. However, they generally assume that the scope and authority of an AI system have already been defined. Relatively little attention has been given to how authority should be allocated, how exposure should be designed, or how authority should expand over time.

This paper introduces SCOPE (Staged Control of Progressive Exposure), a framework for governing authority as a deliberate design process. SCOPE introduces five primary concepts: the Authority Progression Model, which describes how authority evolves within AI-enabled workflows; Exposure, reframed as a design property rather than a post hoc risk assessment; Authority Topology, which governs how authority is distributed across workflows and systems; Expansion Gates, which control authority growth through explicit evidence requirements; and the Scope Ledger, which provides a persistent governance record of authority evolution.

The framework identifies Constraint authority, where systems shape available options and decision spaces, as a critical governance threshold that often precedes autonomous action. By making authority, exposure, and scope expansion explicit design variables, SCOPE provides a practical methodology for governing how AI systems gain influence within organisations.

Rather than replacing existing governance approaches, SCOPE occupies a complementary design layer focused on authority allocation and authority governance before and throughout deployment.
